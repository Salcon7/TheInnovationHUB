Challenge 02: Discover Africa

We chose to tell a story about the Sandawe Tribe who lives in the central part of Tanzania, discussing amazing facts, misconceptions, problems, and uniqueness of the community using a creative media format. Historically, the Sandawe people have been hunter-gatherers. Though many have moved toward agriculture in recent years, their traditional way of life remains deeply connected to the forest and hunting practices. They use traditional methods for hunting, like bows and arrows. The Sandawe speak a language known as Sandawe, which is part of the Khoisan language family. This is one of the few languages in the world that uses click consonants. The Khoisan languages are best known for their characteristic use of these clicks, which is a rare feature among the world’s languages.

Challenge 03: Help-Lab

We contributed to raising funds for health insurance for two people living in Gasabo District through Musave Dispensary. We had the opportunity to sit down and ask Mukarukundo Fanny and Kampire Josephine about their health. They shared some profound insights with us. According to them, being healthy is the greatest wealth one can possess. Despite their family responsibilities and limited resources, they strive to invest in their health to the best of their abilities. In an effort to support their determination, we decided to raise funds and pay for their Mutuelle de Santé, the medical insurance used predominantly by the lower-income population in Rwanda. The gratitude expressed by Fanny and Josephine was overwhelming. They were on the brink of hopelessness, and our timely intervention gave them renewed hope.

Challenge 04: Hunt For Treasure

This challenge involved reaching out to entrepreneurs who are doing great, especially aligning with our G.C.G.O. For our case, we visited Cafe Envision, a startup with a mission in job creation. Cafe Envision is a cafe and event space in Kigali, Rwanda serving craft foods, drinks, and great vibes in their artistically designed way. During the COVID-19 pandemic, they observed a significant increase in unemployment, particularly among the youth. Many capable individuals found themselves on the streets, in need of opportunities to engage in economic activities. Inspired to make a positive impact, they decided to start a business that adhered to WHO COVID-19 regulations.

Problem & Mission Statement

Mission Statement

To reduce unemployment and empower low-income youth in Rwanda by providing job placement services, skill development programs, government initiative support, and entrepreneurship resources. We aim to enhance the country's GDP, improve living standards, and maximize the potential of young talents.

Problem Statement

According to the National Institute of Statistics of Rwanda, 21.97% of young people aged 18 to 24 in Kigali, Rwanda, are affected by a lack of job opportunities, which is a slight decrease from 2022. The key challenges that they face are limited job market access, lack of adequate experience, and skills mismatch between workforce and job demands. This not only affects individuals but also the economy in general and this problem is consistence due to factors such as an increase in population and economic fluctuations.

Get Involved

Invite visitors to explore more.

On the Home page of Ask Mufasa!, you will be prompted to do two things interchangeably, either to upload your existing one so that our system can work with it or create one if you don't have.

When you choose to create a CV with Ask Mufasa!, you will be guided to explore various templates and choose the template that you are comfortable with.

The moment you select a template to use, it will directly take you to a page where you will be required to draft your details, and, in the end, your CV will be complete and ready to download.

After you have downloaded your CV or have an existing one, you can use the upload tab in Ask Mufasa to upload your CV to our system, then click the Upload button.

Our system acts as a skills bridge. Ask Mufasa! extracts important information from your CV, such as work experience and skills, to match them with 21st-century competitive skills in the labor market.

A pie chart will appear after clicking Compare My Skills, giving you access to comparison results and showing which skills you may be missing.

You will then decide to start working with different modules based on your results. This helps you cover the skills gap while tracking your progress as you unlock each module.
